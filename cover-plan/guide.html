Cover & Release Planner — User Guide

Wallscourt Farm Academy  ·  How to use and manage the weekly planner

Overview

The Cover & Release Planner is a shared web page that shows who is out of class each week, which sessions they are released for, and who is covering them. It can be opened on any device — laptop, iPad, or phone — and any changes made on one device are visible on all others after a refresh.

There are two parts to the system:

  • The Google Sheet — holds all the staff, term, and event information that drives the planner. This is where you set up and maintain the data.
  • The Planner — the web page you use week to week to record who is out and who is covering.

Part 1  ·  The Google Sheet

The Google Sheet is the backbone of the planner. It contains five tabs, each with a specific purpose. Most of this is set up once at the start of the year and only needs updating when something changes.

The five tabs

TabWhat it does
StaffEvery member of staff, their role, year group, working days, and their regular release entitlements (e.g. PPA, ECT1). This is used to build the left-hand Teachers panel and the right-hand Cover Staff panel in the planner.
TermsThe start date of every teaching week in the year. The planner uses this to know which dates belong to which week.
EventsOne-off events that block out days or parts of days — for example, INSET days, bank holidays, or school trips. These show as coloured banners in the planner and prevent slots being added on those days.
SchoolEventsRecurring weekly events that appear as informational banners in the grid — for example, Staff Meeting on Monday PM, or Y4 Sports on Friday AM. These do not require cover; they just remind you what is happening.
RecurringThe regular weekly or fortnightly release slots that auto-fill each week — for example, every teacher's PPA session, or an ECT1 release on Thursday PM. New weeks are seeded from this tab automatically when you navigate to them.

Day to day use

Part 2  ·  Using the Planner

Open the planner at the address above. It loads the current week automatically and shows all staff with their weekly release entitlements, ready for you to build the schedule.

The three panels

Left panel — Teachers & Non-Class Based

Everyone who can be released from class. Drag a person from here into the grid to mark them as out for that session.

Centre — The week grid

Five columns (Mon–Fri) and five rows (Before School, AM, Lunch, PM, After School). Each cell shows who is out and who is covering.

Right panel — Cover Staff

Everyone available to provide cover. Drag a cover person from here onto a TBC slot in the grid to assign them.

Today's column

The current day's column is highlighted in blue so you can see at a glance what is happening today.

Adding a release slot

1
Find the teacher in the left panel

All class teachers are listed at the top, followed by non-class based staff (SENCO, Phase Leads, SLT) below the divider.

2
Drag them into the correct cell

Hold and drag the teacher's card across to the session and day you want. A coloured highlight shows where you can drop it. Let go to place the slot.

3
Check or change the reason

Each slot shows a small dropdown with the reason (e.g. PPA, ECT1). The planner pre-selects the most appropriate reason based on that teacher's entitlements. Change it if needed.

Part-time staff: If a teacher only works certain days, you will not be able to drag them into days they are not in school — those days are blocked.

Assigning cover

1
Look for the + TBC box

Any slot without cover assigned shows a pale dashed box marked + TBC.

2
Drag a cover person from the right panel onto the TBC box

The box highlights when you are over it. Drop to assign. The cover person's name and avatar appear in the slot, coloured to match the teacher's year group.

Removing a slot or cover

  • To remove a release slot entirely — click the × button on the right of the slot card.
  • To remove cover only (revert to TBC) — click the small × next to the cover person's name.

Entitlement pips and the green tick

Each teacher card in the left panel shows small coloured dots (pips) next to their entitlements:

  • Filled blue pip — that session has been scheduled in the grid this week
  • Empty pip — still to be scheduled
  • Red pip — more sessions have been scheduled than the entitlement allows
  • Green ✓ tick — all of that person's entitlements for the week are fully scheduled

Use these to check you have not missed anyone and have not accidentally double-booked.

Year group colours

Cards in the grid are colour-coded by the year group of the teacher who is out. This makes it easy to spot at a glance which class is affected.

School event banners

Some cells show a coloured banner at the top — for example Staff Meeting or Y4 Sports. These come from the SchoolEvents tab and are informational only. They appear every week and do not need to be added manually.

Navigating weeks

Use the ← Previous and Next → buttons at the top to move between weeks. The week label (e.g. T5W3) and the Mon–Fri dates are shown for the selected week. Today's week is the default when you open the planner.

Recurring slots

When you navigate to a week that has not been set up yet, it is automatically pre-filled with all the recurring slots from the Recurring tab. This saves you re-entering the same PPA and release sessions every week. You can then add, move, or remove slots as needed for that specific week.

Reset week

The Reset week button (top right of the grid) clears all manually made changes for the current week and restores it to the recurring slots only. Use this if a week has got into a mess and you want to start fresh.

Reset is permanent — any cover assignments or one-off slots you have added for that week will be lost. You cannot undo a reset.

Copy to next week

The Copy → next week button copies the entire current week's layout — including all cover assignments — into the following week. Useful if a week is similar to the last and you only need to make a few changes rather than building from scratch.

Locking the planner

The lock button (padlock icon, top right) switches the planner into read-only mode. When locked, slots cannot be moved, added, or removed, and reason dropdowns are disabled. This is useful when you want to share the screen or hand a device to someone else without risking accidental changes. Tap the padlock again to unlock.

Cross-device

Part 3  ·  Using on multiple devices

The planner saves its state to a shared location (Google Apps Script) whenever you make a change. This means the same schedule is available on any device.

How syncing works

  • Any change you make — adding a slot, assigning cover, removing a slot — is saved automatically within a second or two.
  • Other devices do not update live. To see the latest version on another device, refresh the page.
  • If there is no internet connection, your changes are saved locally on the device and will sync next time you are online.
A good habit: if you have been making changes on the laptop, refresh the planner on the iPad before handing it to someone else — that way they see your latest version.

Who should make changes

The planner is designed for one person to manage at a time. If two people make changes on different devices simultaneously, the last one to save will overwrite the other's changes. For day-to-day use this is not a problem — simply agree who is managing the schedule for the week.

Common tasks

Part 4  ·  Common tasks

Adding a new member of staff

1
Add a row in the Staff tab of the Google Sheet

Fill in Name, Role, YearGroup (if a class teacher), WorkingDays (if part-time), and any entitlements (Ent1, Ent1Sessions, Ent1Freq etc.).

2
If they also provide cover, fill in CoverRole, Initials, and Colour

They will then appear in the right-hand Cover Staff panel as well.

3
Refresh the planner

The new person will appear immediately.

Setting up recurring slots at the start of term

1
Update the Recurring tab if anything has changed

For example, if a teacher's PPA day has moved, update their row. If a new ECT has joined, add their row.

2
Navigate to the first week of the new term in the planner

That week will auto-populate with all the recurring slots. Check they are correct, assign cover, and you are ready.

Adding a one-off absence

1
Navigate to the correct week
2
Drag the teacher into the correct session cell
3
Change the reason to Other (or the appropriate reason)

Use the dropdown on the slot card.

4
Assign cover if needed

Drag from the right-hand cover panel onto the TBC box.

Adding a recurring school event (e.g. new standing meeting)

1
Add a row to the SchoolEvents tab

Fill in Day, Session, Label, and optionally a Colour.

2
Refresh the planner

The banner will appear in the correct cell on every week from now on.

Blocking out an INSET day or bank holiday

1
Add a row to the Events tab

Enter the date, a label (e.g. INSET Day), and the type (inset or bank-holiday).

2
Refresh the planner

That day's column will show the event banner and no slots can be added to it.
